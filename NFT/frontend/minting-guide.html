NFT Minting Guide

How to successfully mint NFTs on your local blockchain

Common Errors & Solutions

Error: "Internal JSON-RPC error"

This error typically means one of the following:

  1. MetaMask can't communicate with Ganache properly
  2. The metadata (image) might be too large for the transaction
  3. Your account might not have enough ETH for gas fees

Step-by-Step Guide to Successful Minting

Step 1: Prepare Your Image

Key point: Image size matters when storing on-chain!

Before trying to mint, use our Image Compression Tool to check if your image is suitable for on-chain storage.

Best practices:

  • Keep images under 100KB before compression
  • Use simple images with fewer details
  • JPG format often compresses better than PNG for photos
Step 2: Verify Your Blockchain Connection

Make sure:

  • Ganache is running on port 7545
  • MetaMask is connected to Ganache (Network ID: 5777, Chain ID: 1337)
  • Your MetaMask account has at least 0.1 ETH for gas fees
  • The correct contract address is set in the app

Clear Contract Address if you're having connection issues.

Step 3: Mint with Proper Values

When minting:

  • Keep the name short (under 50 characters)
  • Keep the description concise (under 200 characters)
  • Use the compressed image from Step 1
  • Watch the debug panel for detailed information during minting
Step 4: Troubleshooting

If minting fails:

  1. Check the debug panel for specific error messages
  2. Try a smaller image
  3. Make sure you have enough ETH in your account
  4. Try restarting Ganache and redeploying the contract
  5. Clear your browser cache and local storage

Understanding On-Chain Storage Limitations

Ethereum and similar blockchains have transaction size limits. Storing data directly on-chain:

  • Is expensive (gas costs)
  • Has size limitations (usually around 24KB per transaction)
  • Is permanent (cannot be modified once minted)

For production NFT projects, consider using IPFS or another decentralized storage solution for larger media files.
